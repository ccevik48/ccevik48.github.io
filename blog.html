This is my first blog post! Here, I will post updates about relevant events that may or may not deal with my professional experience. As an introductory post, I will discuss the time my graduation cap was mistaken for an explosive.(Original Instagram post) (Original WSVN Article and Interview)

I had recently graduated from FIU in Computer Engineering in the Summer of 2019. As a way to decorate my cap, I thought it would be cool and fitting with my major if I did something that showcased some "ability", even though this project was very simple. I wired a seven segment display to an Arduino Uno and programmed it to display between "FIU" and "2019". As I approached security, I was immediately brought to the side and my cap and phone were seized. After short interrogation I was let go and continued to walk, but not before my phone was given back; I was told I would be given my cap back, but after graduation, I was told that it was destroyed and given a new cap, most likely due to avoid any sort of risk. While I was probably acting a bit naive, I should've seen this coming, though just hours before I was at an engineering induction ceremony and had no complaints whatsoever. Oh well, no one was harmed and everything went back to normal.

Lesson learned: never bring an Arduino to a public event where people can assume the worst. (fake bomb scare).
